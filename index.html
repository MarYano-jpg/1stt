Marvic Leonen

Information

Marvic Mario Victor F. Leonen is a Filipino lawyer, professor, and former Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the Philippines. He is best known for his work in constitutional law, indigenous peoplesâ€™ rights, and peace negotiations.

Background and Education

  • Born on December 29, 1962, in the Philippines.
  • Earned his law degree from the University of the Philippines (UP) College of Law.
  • Completed his Master of Laws (LL.M.) from Columbia University in the U.S.

Academic and Legal Career

  • Became a professor at UP College of Law, specializing in constitutional law and environmental law.
  • Served as Dean of UP College of Law from 2008 to 2011.
  • Advocated for indigenous peoples' rights and was involved in drafting laws on their behalf.

Role in the Bangsamoro Peace Process

  • Appointed as Chief Negotiator for the Philippine government in peace talks with the Moro Islamic Liberation Front (MILF).
  • Led negotiations that resulted in the Framework Agreement on the Bangsamoro (2012), a major step in establishing the Bangsamoro Autonomous Region in Muslim Mindanao (BARMM).

Supreme Court Appointment

  • Appointed as Associate Justice of the Supreme Court in 2012 by President Benigno Aquino III.
  • Served as Chairperson of the 2020 Bar Examinations, where he introduced significant reforms, including digital testing.

Retirement

Retired early on December 29, 2022, on his 60th birthday, a year before his mandatory retirement age of 70.

Leonen is widely respected for his legal intellect, independence, and advocacy for marginalized sectors in Philippine society.
